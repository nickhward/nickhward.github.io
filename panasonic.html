NLP Data Cleansing Techniques

1. Tokenization

Tokenization is the process of splitting a large paragraph into sentences or words. This is often the first step in text preprocessing.

2. Removing Stop Words

Stop words like 'and', 'the', 'is', etc., are frequently occurring words that don't carry significant meaning on their own. Removing them can help in reducing the dimensionality.

3. Stemming & Lemmatization

These techniques are used to reduce words to their base/root form. For example, 'running' to 'run'. While stemming cuts off prefixes/suffixes, lemmatization considers the context and converts the word to its meaningful base form.

4. Removing Special Characters and Numbers

Special characters and numbers are usually removed from the text data as they generally don't contribute to word semantics.

5. Lowercasing

Converting all characters in the text to lowercase ensures that words like 'Hello' and 'hello' are considered the same.

In summary, cleansing data in NLP is crucial for building efficient and accurate models. Each preprocessing step prepares the text data, making it a more suitable input for models.

Example Model Selection

After conducting a comprehensive evaluation using 5-fold cross-validation for various models, I concluded that an Ensemble method yielded the most promising results. This Ensemble approach combined the strengths of several algorithms, including LightGBM, XGBoost, Lasso, and LassoLars, to ensure robustness and mitigate the potential weaknesses of any single algorithm.
