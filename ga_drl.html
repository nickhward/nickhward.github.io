Deep Reinforcement Learning Hyperparameter Optimization Utilizing Genetic Algorithms

Leveraged genetic algorithm optimization for hyperparameters within OpenAI deep reinforcement learning environments, yielding a significant, albeit variable, enhancement in task convergence speeds. The proposed method is called GA-DRL

Technology Stack:

  • Python
  • OpenAI Robotic Environments
  • Custom Robotic Environments
  • RVIS
  • ROS
  • Deep Reinforcement Learning
  • Genetic Algorithm

Pick and Place Task

The GA-DRL approach converges by epoch 30, whereas the standard DRL method takes until epoch 80. This indicates that, with our proposed hyperparameter selection technique, convergence is achieved 2.67 times more quickly than when using the original hyperparameters.

Pushing to Target Task

The GA-DRL approach converges by epoch 10, whereas the standard DRL method takes until epoch 20. This indicates that, with our proposed hyperparameter selection technique, convergence is achieved two times more quickly than when using the original hyperparameters.

Simulation GA-DRL Training

To expedite the training process and achieve efficiency, we utilized simulated environments for tasks. This approach allowed us to accelerate movements without being constrained by the limitations of real motors and related hardware.

Simulation to Reality Inference

We're able to store the model and subsequently apply it to the real-world robot, transitioning from the simulation. The results in practice are impressively effective.
